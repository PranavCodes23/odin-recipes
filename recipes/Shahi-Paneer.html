Shahi Paneer


Description

Shahi Paneer is a rich, aromatic, and creamy North Indian curry featuring succulent cubes of Indian cottage cheese dipped in a velvety gravy made of nuts, onions, and yogurt. This step-by-step recipe yields a restaurant-style finish ideal for serving with butter naan or jeera pulao.


Ingredients

  • Onion: 1 large (roughly chopped)
  • Cashew nuts: 15 whole
  • Almonds: 7 pieces
  • Melon seeds (Magaj): 1 tbsp (optional)
  • Garlic: 3-4 cloves
  • Ginger: 1 inch piece
  • Green chilies: 2 pieces
  • Paneer: 250 grams (cubed)
  • Ghee / Butter: 2 tbsp
  • Bay leaf (Tej patta): 1
  • Green cardamom: 2
  • Black cardamom: 1
  • Cinnamon: 1 inch stick
  • Cloves: 2-3
  • Shahi Jeera (Black Cumin): ½ tsp
  • Kashmiri red chili powder: ½ tsp
  • Coriander powder: ½ tsp
  • Garam masala: ¼ tsp
  • Yogurt (Curd): 2 tbsp (well whisked)
  • Fresh cream: 2 tbsp
  • Saffron (Kesar): 6-8 strands (soaked in 1 tbsp warm milk)
  • Kasuri methi: a generous pinch
  • Sugar: ¼ tsp (to balance flavors)
  • Salt: to taste

Steps for Prepration

  1. Prepare the Base Paste
    1. Put the chopped onion, cashews, almonds, melon seeds, ginger, garlic, green chilies, and ½ cup of water into a saucepan.
    2. Boil on medium heat for 8 to 10 minutes until the onions turn soft and translucent.
    3. Drain the excess water but save it for later use in the gravy.
    4. Allow the boiled ingredients to cool completely, then blend them into a completely smooth, fine paste.
  2. Temper the Aromatics
    1. Melt the ghee or butter in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat.
    2. Add the bay leaf, green cardamom, black cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, and shahi jeera.
    3. Sauté for 30 seconds until the spices release their aroma.
  3. Cook the Gravy Base
    1. Pour the prepared white onion-nut paste into the pan with the spices.
    2. Cook on medium-low heat for 4 to 5 minutes, stirring continuously, until the mixture thickens and starts leaving the sides of the pan.
    3. Lower the flame completely. Add the whisked yogurt slowly while stirring continuously to prevent curdling. Cook for 2 minutes.
    4. Stir in the Kashmiri red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, salt, and sugar.
  4. Simmer and Finish
    1. Pour in the reserved boiling water (or plain warm water) to adjust the consistency to a thick, pouring gravy. Bring it to a gentle simmer.
    2. Gently drop in the raw paneer cubes. (Tip: If your paneer is firm, soak it in warm water for 10 minutes before adding to keep it soft).
    3. Simmer on low heat for 3 to 4 minutes so the paneer absorbs the flavors.
    4. Turn off the heat. Stir in the fresh cream, saffron-infused milk, and crush the kasuri methi between your palms directly over the curry.

    Serve warm garnished with an extra swirl of fresh cream and coriander leaves if desired.
